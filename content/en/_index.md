{{% details title="üêá Easter Puja- 5 April" closed="true" %}}

For Easter Puja, Yogis can bring eggs and cakes and various Easter sweets and treats!
We could organize an Easter egg hunt for kids or do fun joyful theatrics with breaking an egg.

On this day the Moon will be in a Waning Gibbous.
During this phase the Moon can be seen in the early morning daylight hours on the western horizon.
This is the first phase after the Full Moon occurs.

The international Puja is hosted in Romania and the hosting countries are Romania, Moldova & Turkey

{{% details title="üôè Shri Adi Shakti Puja - 28 June" closed="true" %}}

On this day the Moon will be in a Waxing Gibbous phase.
Visible through most of the night sky setting a few hours before sunrise.
This phase is when the moon is more than 50% illuminated but not yet a Full Moon.
The phase lasts about 7 days with the moon becoming more illuminated each day until the Full Moon.

The international Puja is hosted in United Kingdom and the hosting countries are
UK, Baltics, Belgium, Finland, France. Iceland, Netherlands, Scandinavian Countries & Spain.

[Volunteer for this Puja](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1Oyv-Sz_d_KV2AoKKokpqg-LnoRiWKFF4I0zFMWm9nyQ/edit?usp=sharing)

{{% details title="üìø Shri Adi Guru Puja - 2 August" closed="true" %}}

The Guru is worshiped after the Devi Puja. During the Guru Puja, it is common to offer
sandalwood powder at the Feet of the Guru. The Guru is adorned with a green sari and an
orange shall. Tulsi (holy basil), fig, Guru's staff and watering can can be offered at the Puja.
This is the day that Shri Mataji's Paduka can be worshiped.

On this day the Moon will be in a Waning Gibbous.
During this phase the Moon can be seen in the early morning daylight hours on the western horizon.
This is the first phase after the Full Moon occurs.

The international Puja is hosted in Cabella and the hosting countries are
Italy, Bulgaria, Israel, Lebanon, Oman, Portugal, Romania, Russia, Turkey, UAE,
and other Middle Eastern countries.

[Volunteer for this Puja](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/10zemyvUDZ0MqYUKDkAbmKQq-EwYToR2TCbIvd0GOk8k/edit?usp=sharing)

{{% details title="ü¶ö Shri Krishna Puja - 6 September" closed="true" %}}

Shri Krishna is worshiped after the Devi Puja. It is common to offer sweet butter to Shri Krishna.
This butter is essentially really hard whipped cream and can be made by the Yogis. In addition
Tulsi (holy Basil) plant, yogurt, honey, peacock feathers, flute, Sudarshan chakra
and conch can be offered as part of the Shri Krishna Puja. Shri Krishna Puja is commonly
followed by the Raksha Bandhan ceremony.

On this day the Moon will be in a Waning Crescent phase.
This phase is best viewed just before the sunrise in the western sky.
In this phase the Moon‚Äôs illumination is growing smaller each day until the New Moon.

The international Puja is hosted in Cabella and the hosting countries are
North, Central & South America, Albania, Croatia, Macedonia, Poland, Romania & Serbia

{{% details title="üêò Shri Ganesha Puja - 12 September" closed="true" %}}

It is common to offer hibiscus flower and Durva grass to Shri Ganesha.

On this day the Moon will be in a Waxing Crescent Phase.
Best seen in the west after the sun dips below the horizon at sunset.
This is the first Phase after the New Moon and is a great time
to see the features of the moon's surface.

The international Puja is hosted in Cabella and the hosting countries are
Italy, Andorra, Australia, China, Hong Kong, India, Indonesia, Japan, Malaysia,
New Zealand, Philippines, Russia, Singapore, Spain, Taiwan, Thailand & Vietnam.

{{% details title="‚öîÔ∏è Shri Navaratri Puja & Havan - 18 October" closed="true" %}}

On this day the Moon will be in a First Quarter phase.
This phase occurs roughly 7 days after the New Moon when the moon is
one quarter of the way through its orbit around the earth.
Exactly half the moon will be illuminated and half dark.

The international Puja is hosted in Cabella and the hosting countries are
UK, Belarus, Cyprus, Israel, Switzerland & Ukraine.
